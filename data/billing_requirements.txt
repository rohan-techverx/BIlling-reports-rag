Requirements 1.0
Brokered Customer Billing Report
Document Version: 1.0
Date: February 2026
Status: Current Working Implementation (Baseline)
Prepared By: Techverx Development Team
Purpose
This document defines the current, production-working requirements for the Brokered Customer Billing Report.
These requirements were established through:
Existing production code analysis
Historical behavior
Client meetings and clarifications
Formal business rules were not fully documented at the start of development. This document serves as the authoritative baseline (v1.0) against which future changes, enhancements, and fixes will be evaluated.

1. Customer Contract Dependencies
REQ-1.1 Contract-Centric Billing
The brokered customer billing report is fully dependent on the customer contract.
Business Rules
All billing data originates from customer contract configuration.
A billing report cannot exist without a customer contract.
Contracts are the single source of truth for services, locations, pricing, and billing schedule.
Technical Rules
Services are queried from CustomerContractService.
Services must be linked to a customer contract.
Contract must have billing_date populated for services to appear.

REQ-1.2 Effective Service Date
The contract effective service date defines the contract start.
Business Rules
Marks the beginning of the contractual relationship.
Serves as the baseline for all time-based calculations.
Technical Rules
Stored in CustomerContract.effective_date.
Used as fallback when service start dates are missing.
Used for Annual Price Increase (API) calculations.

REQ-1.3 Contract Term (Months)
Contract term is defined in months.
Business Rules
12 months = 1 year
60 months = 5 years
Technical Rules
Stored in CustomerContract.term (integer).
Used to calculate contract end date.

REQ-1.4 Contract End Date Calculation
Contract end date is derived, not manually entered.
Business Rules
Defines the contractual expiration point.
Does not terminate billing visibility.
Technical Rules
contract_end_date = effective_date + term (months)


REQ-1.5 Post-Expiration Billing Visibility
Billing continues after contract expiration.
Business Rules
Contracts continue on a month-to-month basis after expiration.
Billing data must remain visible after contract end date.
Prevents service interruption caused by administrative expiration.
Technical Rules
No active expiration filter in billing report queries.
Previously excluded behavior has been removed.

2. Customer Billing Bucket (Billing Date)
REQ-2.1 Billing Date Selection
Each contract must define a billing date.
Business Rules
Allowed values: 1st, 15th, 30th
Defines when billing occurs each month.
Technical Rules
Stored in CustomerContract.billing_date.day.
Required field.

REQ-2.2 Missing Billing Date Exclusion
Contracts without billing dates are excluded.
Business Rules
Prevents undefined or inconsistent billing.
Technical Rules
Filter: contract__billing_date__isnull=False.

REQ-2.3 Multi-Month Billing Multiplication
Billing date determines charge frequency across multi-month periods.
Business Rules
Each occurrence of the billing date generates a charge.


Example:

 Period Jan–Mar with billing date = 15 → 2 charges (Jan 15, Feb 15).


Technical Rules
Services multiplied by count of billing dates in period.

3. Location & Service Management
REQ-3.1 Location Association
Contracts may contain multiple service locations.
Business Rules
One contract can bill multiple locations.
Each location may have unique services and pricing.
Technical Rules
Linked via CustomerContractLocation.

REQ-3.2 Location Visibility
Locations without services are excluded.
Business Rules
Empty locations should not appear in reports.
Technical Rules
Locations must have at least one active monthly service.

REQ-3.3 Zero-Priced Monthly Services (Visibility Rule)
Monthly services with $0.00 pricing must still display.
Business Rules
Monthly services define the contractual service structure.
Zero-priced monthly services must remain visible.
Technical Rules
Required change: remove .exclude(price=0) from available_fixed_services().
Continue excluding price IS NULL.

REQ-3.4 Service Description Format
Service descriptions must follow a consistent format.
Format
<Service Type> - <Size> - <Container> - Pickup: <Frequency>

Example
MSW - 1 - 8 - Pickup: 3x/week


REQ-3.5 Missing Service Start Date
Services without a start date inherit contract start date.
Technical Rule
start = service.effective_date_from OR contract.effective_date


REQ-3.6 Missing Service End Date
Services without an end date never expire.
Business Rules
Service remains active until explicitly ended.

REQ-3.7 Service Expiration Handling
Expired services do not appear in future billing periods.
Technical Rule
Service excluded if effective_date_to < billing_period_start.

REQ-3.8 Monthly vs On-Demand Services
Different display rules apply by service type.
Business Rules
Monthly services: Always displayed (even $0.00).
On-demand services: Displayed only if price > $0.00.

REQ-3.9 Management Fees
Location management fees must be billed.
Business Rules
Fixed fee per billing occurrence.
Technical Rules
management_fee × number_of_billing_dates


4. Multi-Location Add-On Charges
REQ-4.1 Multi-Location Groups
Multi-location groups contain on-demand services.

REQ-4.2 Zero-Priced On-Demand Exclusion
On-demand services with $0.00 pricing are excluded.

REQ-4.3 Location-Specific Application
On-demand services apply only to selected locations.

5. Percentage-Based Charges
REQ-5.1 Percentage Charge Formula
Percentage Charge = Base Amount × Percentage Rate


REQ-5.2 – REQ-5.5 Date Validity
Percentage charges:
Activate on start date
Expire on end date
Must overlap billing period
Use contract dates if NULL

6. Annual Price Increases (APIs)
REQ-6.1 Selective Application
Customers select which contract years apply an annual price increase. Only selected years trigger a percentage adjustment.
REQ-6.2 Custom Percentage Caps
Each selected year has its own configurable percentage cap (e.g., Year 2 = 4%, Year 3 = 6%). Percentages are not required to be uniform across years.
REQ-6.3 Compounding Calculation
Each increase is calculated on the previous year's final price—not the original base price. This creates a compounding effect across consecutive selected years.
REQ-6.4 Skipped Year Handling
Unselected years freeze the price at the last calculated amount. No increase applies, but the accumulated price from prior selected years carries forward unchanged.
REQ-6.5 Year Determination
The system calculates applicable APIs using the calendar year difference between the contract effective date and the billing month (e.g., effective date 03/01/2024 to billing month 03/2025 = 1 year passed = Year 2 rates apply if selected).
REQ-6.6 Future API Exclusion
Only APIs with an effective date on or before the billing month are included in calculations. Future-dated APIs are ignored until their effective date is reached.

7. Pass-Through Charges
REQ-7.1 Source
Pass-through charges originate from hauler invoices.

REQ-7.2 Period Overlap Requirement
Only invoices overlapping the pass-through period are included.

REQ-7.3 Margin Application
Default margin: 20%, configurable per report.

REQ-7.4 Monthly Service Dependency
Pass-throughs only appear for locations with monthly services.

REQ-7.5 Hauler Contract Percentages
Optional additional percentages may apply and compound.

8. Report Generation & Display
REQ-8.1 Header Content
Report ID, date, periods, and margin must be displayed.

REQ-8.2 Location Grouping
Locations displayed with ID, address, and store number.

REQ-8.3 Service Listing
Each service shows description and final calculated amount.

REQ-8.4 Summary Section
Summary includes:
Fuel & Environmental Charges
Subtotal
Management Fee
GTS Fee ($0.00)
Grand Total
Hauler Cost
Net Profit

REQ-8.5 Margin Disclosure
Margin note displayed when applicable.

9. Hauler Cost Calculation
REQ-9.1 Eligible Locations
Hauler costs are calculated exclusively for service locations where service_performed contains "Brokered". Non-brokered locations are excluded from hauler cost calculations.
REQ-9.2 Invoice Selection Criteria
Invoices are included only when:
invoice.service_from ≤ billing period end date
invoice.service_to ≥ billing period start date
Both service dates are not null
invoice.is_duplicate = False
REQ-9.3 Active Contract Validation
Invoices are excluded if the associated contract was not active during the invoice service period. Contract status is verified against the invoice date range—inactive or expired contracts do not generate hauler costs even if invoices exist.
REQ-9.4 Proration Methodology
Invoice amounts are prorated based on calendar days overlap:
Prorated Amount = Invoice Amount × (Overlap Days ÷ Total Invoice Days)

Where:
Overlap Days: Count of days where invoice service period intersects with billing period
Total Invoice Days: Count of days in the invoice's full service period
REQ-9.5 Multi-Invoice Aggregation
When multiple invoices overlap with the billing period for the same location, each invoice is prorated independently and summed to determine total hauler cost for that location.

10. Output & Delivery
REQ-10.1 Data Processing Flow
Contracts → Services → Dates → Pricing → Grouping → Summary.

REQ-10.2 PDF Generation
Billing report generated as PDF using dynamic template.

Questions & Answers (Brokered Billing Report)

Q1: What determines whether a customer appears in the brokered billing report?
A:
A customer appears only if they have at least one active customer contract with a defined billing date and at least one billable service within the selected billing period.

Q2: Can a single customer have multiple brokered billing reports for the same period?
A:
No. A customer receives one brokered billing report per billing period, regardless of the number of contracts or locations included.

Q3: Why are contracts without services excluded from the report?
A:
Contracts without services cannot generate charges. Including them would create empty or misleading billing output.

Q4: How does the system determine which services are billable for a period?
A:
A service is billable if its effective date range overlaps with the billing period and it meets pricing and service-type visibility rules.

Q5: Why do some services appear with the same charge repeated multiple times?
A:
Monthly services are charged once per billing date occurrence. If multiple billing dates fall within the billing period, the service charge is multiplied accordingly.

Q6: Are services billed if they start mid-month?
A:
Yes. Services that start during the billing period are billed if their effective start date occurs on or before a billing date within the period.

Q7: Are services billed if they end mid-month?
A:
Yes. Services remain billable through their effective end date, provided they overlap with at least one billing date in the period.

Q8: How are location-level totals calculated?
A:
Each location's total is calculated by summing all applicable monthly services, on-demand services, management fees, pass-through charges, and applicable percentage-based charges.

Q9: Why might two locations under the same contract have different totals?
A:
Each location may have different services, pricing, management fees, on-demand usage, and hauler costs, resulting in different totals.

Q10: How are management fees applied in brokered billing?
A:
Management fees are fixed charges applied per location and multiplied by the number of billing date occurrences in the billing period.

Q11: Why are some on-demand services missing from the report?
A:
On-demand services are shown only when their price is greater than $0.00 and when they apply to at least one selected service location.

Q12: How are multi-location add-on services handled?
A:
Multi-location add-ons are applied only to the locations explicitly selected in the add-on configuration and are calculated independently per location.

Q13: Why are pass-through charges not shown for some locations?
A:
Pass-through charges appear only for locations that have active monthly services and qualifying hauler invoices overlapping the billing period.

Q14: How does the system prevent duplicate hauler charges?
A:
Invoices marked as duplicates are excluded from calculations to prevent double billing.

Q15: Why are hauler costs prorated instead of fully charged?
A:
Invoices often span a longer date range than the billing period. Proration ensures only the portion overlapping the billing period is charged.

Q16: How is net profit calculated in brokered billing reports?
A:
Net profit is calculated as the difference between total billed charges and total hauler costs for each location, then aggregated at the report level.

Q17: Can historical billing reports change after generation?
A:
No. Once generated, a billing report reflects the data as of that point in time. Subsequent contract or service changes do not retroactively alter past reports.

Q18: What happens if a service price is updated after a report is generated?
A:
The updated price applies only to future billing periods. Previously generated reports remain unchanged.

Q19: Why does the report still show locations that are no longer owned by the customer?
A:
Locations remain visible if they are still linked to an active contract. Ownership changes must be reflected by updating or removing the location from the contract.

Q20: What is considered the source of truth when discrepancies are found?
A:
The customer contract and this Brokered Billing Report requirements document (v1.0) together define the authoritative source of truth for billing behavior.

✅ Enhancement: Fuel & Environmental Charges Updated to Include Pass-Through Charges
In Version 1.1, the calculation logic for Fuel & Environmental (F&E) percentage-based charges has been updated.
Previous Behavior (v1.0)
Fuel & Environmental charges were applied only to core service charges.
Pass-through charges were excluded from the fuel calculation base.
Updated Behavior (v1.1)
Fuel & Environmental percentage charges are now applied to:
Monthly service charges
On-demand service charges
Pass-through charges (including overages and invoice-derived charges)
Updated Calculation Logic
Fuel & Environmental Base = Service Charges + On-Demand Charges + Pass-Through Charges
Fuel Charge = Fuel Base × Applicable Percentage

Business Impact
Ensures consistent percentage application across all revenue-generating service components.
Aligns billing output with updated business policy.
Produces higher but accurate Fuel & Environmental totals when pass-through charges exist.
Improves transparency in subtotal calculations.
Scope of Change
Affects REQ-8.4 Summary Section.
Introduces REQ-8.4.1 Fuel & Environmental Charges – Pass-Through Inclusion.
No changes to hauler cost calculations.
No changes to margin application logic.

REQ-8.4.1 Fuel & Environmental Charges – Pass-Through Inclusion
Business Rule
Fuel and environmental percentage charges must be applied not only to the billed services but also to any included pass-through charges.
This ensures the subtotal reflects the combined service and pass-through amounts before percentage adjustments.
Technical Rule
When calculating Fuel & Environmental Charges:
Applicable Amount = Service Charges + Pass-Through Charges
Fuel/Environmental Charge = Applicable Amount × Applicable Percentage

Update report generation logic to include pass-throughs in subtotal before applying percentage-based charges.
Example
Service Charge = $1,000
Pass-Through Charge = $200
Fuel Surcharge = 5%
Subtotal for Fuel Surcharge = (1,000 + 200) × 5% = $60


This aligns the requirements with your recent feature in #640, ensuring clarity that pass-throughs contribute to percentage-based charges like fuel/environmental fees.

Consulting Customer Billing Report
Document Version: 1.1
Date: February 12, 2026
Status: Baseline – Approved for Production
Prepared By: Techverx Development Team

1. Purpose (Updated – v1.1)
This document defines the requirements for the Consulting Customer Billing Report, with a focus on savings-driven reporting while respecting user-selected service periods.
Key updates in v1.1:
The report must now dynamically respect the billing date range selected by the user.
Static fields (Benchmark Price, Shared Saving Percentage) remain unchanged and are configuration-driven.
Dynamic fields (Current Price, Net Savings, GTS Fee, Location Subtotal, Summary totals) are calculated strictly using invoice data within the selected service period.
Charges that partially overlap the selected date range must be prorated.
The report is:
Location-focused
Savings-driven
Dynamic according to the selected billing period

2. Scope
Report Type: consulting_invoice
Eligibility: Customers with customer_type = "consulting"
Location Dependency: Financial values come from each location record (Benchmark, Current Price, Net Savings, GTS Fee, Percentage-Based Charges, Management Fee)
Management Fee: If specified at the contract-location level, must be displayed
Report Objective: Show savings, GTS Fee, percentage-based charges (if any), Management Fee, net profit, and totals for all contract-linked locations

3. Customer & Contract Rules
3.1 Contract Continuity
Reports are generated based on active contract-location relationships
Contract expiration dates do not automatically remove locations
Month-to-month continuation applies unless explicitly deactivated
3.2 Location Inclusion
All locations linked to the contract must be included
No filtering based on:
$0.00 charges
Savings amount
Minimum thresholds
3.3 Management Fees
Pulled from contract-location record
Displayed per location if defined
Contributes to:
Location Subtotal
Net Profit

4. Report Structure
4.1 Header
Customer Name
Report ID (Sequential if enabled)
Billing Period (monthly_start_date – monthly_end_date)
Report Date (actual generation date)

4.2 Location Details
Each location row includes:
Location Identifier
Address & Store Number
Benchmark Price (Static)
Current Price (Dynamic, filtered by date range)
Net Savings (minimum $0.00)
GTS Fee %
GTS Fee (minimum $0.00)
Percentage-Based Charges (if configured)
Management Fee (if defined)
Location Subtotal

4.3 GTS Fee % Priority
GTS Fee % is determined in the following order:
Shared Saving Percentage
Benchmark Percentage
Default = 50%

5. Calculation Logic
5.1 Net Savings
Net Savings = max(0, Benchmark Price − Current Price)

Negative values converted to $0.00
Label: "Net Saving"
Benchmark Price = Static (configuration)
Current Price = Dynamic (filtered by selected billing period)

5.2 GTS Fee

11. Q&A – Consulting Billing Report
Q1: Why does the report display locations even if charges are low or $0?
A: All contract-linked locations appear, no minimum filters applied.
Q2: How is Current Price sourced?
A: Current Price is dynamic and filtered by the billing period; defaults to $0.00 if no invoices exist.
Q3: What is Net Saving?
A: Net Saving = max(0, Benchmark Price − Current Price)
Q4: How is GTS Fee calculated?
A: GTS Fee = Net Saving × (GTS Fee % / 100) (priority: Shared Saving %, Benchmark %, Default 50%)
Q5: When is Management Fee displayed?
A: Only if defined at contract-location level; included in Location Subtotal & Net Profit
Q6: How is Net Profit calculated?
A: Net Profit = GTS Fee + Management Fee
Q7: Can Net Savings be negative?
A: No. Negative savings = $0.00
Q8: Why are labels changed from "Your Saving"?
A: Standardized to "Net Saving" and removed green highlighting
Q9: Does the report depend on hauler invoices?
A: No. Report is location-based
Q10: Are expired contracts excluded?
A: No. Locations remain if the contract-location relationship is active
Q11: Can locations with $0 Current Price show GTS Fee?
A: Only if Benchmark Price > 0
Q12: How does the report handle multiple locations?
A: All contract-linked locations included; each has own subtotal
Q13: What determines GTS Fee % per location?
A: Shared Saving % → Benchmark % → Default 50%
Q14: What is the Grand Total Payable?
A: Sum of all location subtotals
Q15: How does consulting differ from brokered reports?
A: Savings-driven, location-based; brokered is cost-reconciliation-based
Q16: What if a location has no charges?
A: Current Price defaults to $0.00; Net Saving & GTS Fee calculated accordingly
Q17: Are minimum charge filters applied?
A: No
Q18: Authoritative source of truth?
A: Location record data, contract-location relationship, this document
Q19: Can historical consulting reports change?
A: No. Reports are immutable
Q20: How are report subtotals structured?
Location Subtotal = Current Price + Percentage-Based Charges (if any) + GTS Fee + Management Fee
